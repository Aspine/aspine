Overview

Aspine is a frontend for Aspen that makes it easier for CPSD students to see and predict their grades. For example, you can predict what your grade in a class, and your GPA, would be if you scored better on a test, or got an A on an upcoming project.

Editing assignments

Editing assignments is easy. In the Grades tab, select a class to see its categories and the individual assignment. Selecting a category filters the assignments by that category. Assignment names, categories, scores, and maximum scores can all be modified and your overall grade as well as category subtotals will be based on what you entered.

Adding assignments

Clicking the plus () icon enters a new assignment in the currently selected category, which you can change to your liking to reflect possible upcoming assignments.

Test corrections

Clicking the hammer () icon on an assignment allows you to predict your grade if you are able to redeem a certain percentage of the points you lost on the assignment. For example, if you get a score of 80/100 on a test and your teacher allows you to get 50% of your points back, entering 50% would cause Aspine to recalculate your grade with a score of 90/100 on the test.

Assignment statistics

For classes whose teachers share assignment statistics, clicking the info () icon on an assignment contextualizes your score with a profile of your classmates' scores.

Report cards

In the Reports tab, you can view and download your report cards and progress reports.

Clock

During a school day, the clock (in the top-left corner) shows you the name of your current class and the amount of time remaining until the end of the class, or shows the amount of time until the beginning of school if viewed before school starts.

Importing/exporting grades

Clicking the download () icon lets you export your grades in a machine-readable format that you can store on your computer. You can import these back into Aspen at a later date by clicking the Current Year dropdown menu and selecting Import Data.

GPA type and switching quarters

You can click the GPA Type button to toggle between percentage, unweighted (4.0 scale), and weighted (5.0 scale) GPA. The Current Quarter dropdown shows your GPA for each quarter and also allows you to switch to another quarter to see its grades and assignments.
